ABOUT

Starting my career as an Educator made me see my path as an artist as a bit unconventional. My Architecture background has a significant influence on my work. Colors, shapes, a sense of order, and raw materials carrying on its own textures is something I enjoy being evident in my art.

Art has been a big part of my interests ever since I can remember, but for some reason or another, I did not feel comfortable calling me an artist until a few years ago. Recently I started studying MFA Scenic Design and I found myself on a journey of self-discovery through a continuous process of learning, unlearning, and relearning.

As a Scenic Designer, I appreciate work collaboratively, I keep those conversations and the feedback as a treasure to keep moving and growing in the artistic process. The most enjoyable part of being a Scenic Designer is the process of telling a story through the scenes, having endless possibilities, and aiming the wonder. I start my work with an abstract idea and an open mind. I attempt to combine knowledge and technique with an intuition that guides me during my creative efforts. Being an artist and having the opportunity to frame the ordinary poetically on a stage is what moves me to keep moving forward. I am eager to keep progressing in the artistic journey and excited by what the future brings.
